Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Missile Man of India & Former President

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was an Indian aerospace scientist and the 11th President of India. He played a crucial role in India's missile development and inspired millions with his humility and vision.

Early Life

Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, Kalam came from a humble background. Despite financial hardships, he was a bright student who later graduated in Aerospace Engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology.

Career Highlights

  • Worked at ISRO and DRDO on missile and space projects
  • Played a key role in India’s 1998 nuclear tests
  • Served as the 11th President of India (2002–2007)
  • Authored inspiring books like "Wings of Fire", "Ignited Minds"

Timeline

  • 1931 - Born in Rameswaram
  • 1960 - Joined DRDO
  • 1980 - Led India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III)
  • 1998 - Led Pokhran-II Nuclear Tests
  • 2002 - Became the President of India
  • 2015 - Passed away while giving a lecture at IIM Shillong
